## The Core Challenge

The legacy FJpay app suffered from a confusing user experience, an outdated interface, and a lack of trust-building elements, leading to high user drop-off during critical KYC and money transfer processes.

## A User-Centric Process

To rebuild trust and streamline core functionalities, we adopted a comprehensive design process focused on the specific needs of our target audience: Nepali residents in Japan, often with limited Japanese proficiency.

## Key Learnings

This project reinforced the importance of deep user research, especially for niche audiences. Incorporating cultural cues, such as the localized tagline छिटो छ, सजिलो छ (It's fast, it's easy), proved to be a powerful tool for building trust. Close collaboration with the development team from the component-building stage was crucial for a smooth and accurate handoff.
